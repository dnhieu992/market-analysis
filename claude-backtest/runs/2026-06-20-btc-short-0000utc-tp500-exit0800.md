# BTC SHORT @ 00:00 UTC, TP −$500, force-close 08:00 UTC, no stop

**Date:** 2026-06-20
**Script:** `scripts/run-btc-short-0000-tp500-backtest.ts`
**Window:** 365 days · `1h` candles · $1000 fixed/trade (NO compounding) · fee 0.05%/side
**Context:** user-specified clock strategy — SHORT BTC at the daily open, take profit at a fixed
**$500 price move** (absolute points, not %), hard time-exit at 08:00 UTC, no stop.

## Rule
Every day: SHORT at the OPEN of the 00:00 UTC 1h candle. TP if any candle in 00:00–07:00 trades down
to `entry − $500` → exit there. No stop. Else force-close at 08:00 UTC (open of the 08:00 candle).
Fixed $1000 notional.

## Command
```bash
scripts/run-btc-short-0000-tp500-backtest.ts 365 0.05 1000 500 8
```

## Results

| metric | value |
|--------|------:|
| trades | 365 |
| TP hit (−$500) | 188 (51.5%) |
| forced close | 177 (36 green) |
| gross win / loss | +$1,119.51 / −$993.88 |
| **GROSS P&L** | **+$125.63** (avg +$0.34/trade) |
| total fees | −$365.03 |
| **NET P&L** | **−$239.41** (avg −$0.66/trade) |

## Takeaway

**Small positive gross edge, killed by fees → net −$239.** Shorting BTC over the 00:00→08:00 UTC
window has a slight down-drift: the $500 TP fills on 51.5% of days and gross is **+$126**. But at $1000
notional the round-trip fee is $1/trade → **$365/yr in fees**, which swamps the +$0.34/trade gross edge.

The no-stop + fixed-TP asymmetry hurts: of 177 forced closes only 36 are green, i.e. when the short
goes against you (BTC rallies into 08:00) the loss runs unbounded while the winners are capped at $500.
That's why gross win ($1,120) barely exceeds gross loss ($994) despite a >50% TP-hit rate.

**Verdict: not tradeable as specified.** Same structural problem as the long clock tests: a real but
tiny edge ($0.34/trade) below the fee hurdle ($1/trade at this size). Note size doesn't help — fee is
%-based so larger notional scales fees identically. Levers that could matter: (a) earlier exit (the
long study peaked at 04:00 — worth testing 04:00 for the short too), (b) a 1D-bear trend filter (only
short on down-trend days — the analog of the long-side fix that flipped SOL/BNB net-positive), (c) a
smaller TP for a higher hit rate. As-is, the fee drag dominates.

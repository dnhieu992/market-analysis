# EMA Ribbon 34 / 89 / 200 — backtest

**Date:** 2026-06-16
**Script:** `scripts/run-ema-ribbon-backtest.ts` (new)

## Strategy
Triple-EMA "ribbon" alignment, decision on candle **close**:
- **LONG** when EMA34 > EMA89 > EMA200 (bullish stack)
- **SHORT** when EMA34 < EMA89 < EMA200 (bearish stack)
- **FLAT** otherwise (EMAs tangled / not in order)

Not always-in-market: sits flat while EMAs are tangled. Fee charged per side actually
traded (no fee while flat). $1000 compounded, no leverage.

## Takeaway
The ribbon strategy is **mediocre and inconsistent** across coins on both timeframes.
Only BTC and BNB were net-profitable in both tests; ETH/SOL/XRP lost or barely broke even,
with SOL on D1 deeply negative (-37.65%, 56.96% maxDD). Low win rates (≈26-47% on H4) are
typical of a trend-follow rule — gains depend on a few big trends paying for many small
losers — but here the few winners rarely covered the chop. The EMA200 alignment requirement
delays entries (price has already moved before the full stack forms) and the strict stack
exit gives back a lot at turns. Versus the user's UTBot stop-and-reverse runs, this ribbon
rule is not competitive as-is. Possible improvements to try: looser exit (only require
EMA34/EMA89 cross to flatten, keep EMA200 as long/short filter), or an ATR/price-vs-EMA200
trend filter instead of full stack.
